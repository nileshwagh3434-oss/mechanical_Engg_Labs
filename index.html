🖥️ Aaryabhattas CAD Lab

Computer‑Aided Design & Drafting
📌 What & Why? CAD lab enables 3D modelling, simulation, and engineering drawings. Essential for design validation before manufacturing.
⚡ Need & Importance Industry standard tools (SolidWorks, CATIA) reduce errors, save cost, and accelerate product development.
🧩 Applications Automotive design, aerospace components, tooling, and FEA analysis.
✅ Advantages Visualisation, quick iteration, integration with 3D printing.
⚠️ Disadvantages High software cost, steep learning curve.
🛠️ How to use: Attend induction, follow lab manual, use workstations with licenced software.
📍 When/where: Timetabled sessions + open projects.
🧰 Equipment: 30 HP workstations, 3D printers, plotter, SolidWorks, AutoCAD, ANSYS.
🛡️ Safety: No food/drink, cable management, ergonomic chairs, regular software backups.
💼 Career: Design engineer, CAE analyst, product development.

⚙️ Pascals Automation Lab

Pneumatics, Hydraulics & PLC
📌 What? Industrial automation using pneumatic, hydraulic, and PLC systems. Simulates real factory lines.
🎯 Why & Need: Automation is core to Industry 4.0; hands-on PLC programming and circuit building.
⚙️ Applications: Conveyor control, robotic grippers, packaging machines.
✅ Pros: Real‑world skills, quick troubleshooting. Cons: Air leakage, component cost.
🧑‍🔧 How to use: Follow pneumatic circuit diagrams, use Festo kits, simulate with FluidSIM.
🧰 Equipment: Festo cylinders, Siemens PLC, compressors, solenoid valves.
🛡️ Safety: Lockout/tagout, wear PPE, check pressure before connecting.
💼 Career: Automation engineer, PLC programmer, maintenance.

📡 IoT Lab

Internet of Things for Mechanical Systems
📌 What? Smart sensors, microcontrollers, cloud data acquisition for mechanical systems.
⚡ Need: Predictive maintenance, remote monitoring, digital twin basics.
🧩 Applications: Vibration monitoring, temperature logging, smart factory.
✅ Advantages: Low‑cost, scalable. ⚠️ Disadvantages: cybersecurity, sensor calibration.
🛠️ How: Program ESP32/Arduino, connect sensors, upload to cloud.
🧰 Equipment: NodeMCU, DHT22, vibration sensor, Raspberry Pi.
🛡️ Safety: Low voltage, avoid ESD, secure wiring.
💼 Career: IoT developer, automation consultant.

🖨️ Aaj Abdul Kalam Lab – 3D Printing

Additive Manufacturing & Rapid Prototyping
📌 What? FDM, SLA, resin printing for functional prototypes.
🎯 Why? Fast iteration, complex geometries, reduced waste.
🧩 Applications: Jigs, fixtures, medical models, end‑use parts.
✅ Advantages: Customisation, low‑volume cost. ⚠️ Disadvantages: layer lines, limited strength.
🛠️ How: Design STL, slice, print, post‑process.
🧰 Equipment: 8 FDM, 2 SLA, wash/cure station.
🛡️ Safety: Fume extraction, avoid hot surfaces, use gloves for resin.
💼 Career: Additive manufacturing engineer, design for AM.

💧 Fluid Mechanics Lab

Hydraulics & Fluid Dynamics
📌 What? Study of fluid behaviour, Bernoulli, pipe flow, turbines.
⚡ Need: Design of pumps, piping, hydraulic systems.
🧩 Applications: Water supply, hydro power, HVAC.
✅ Advantages: Visual learning, real data. ⚠️ Disadvantages: setup time, water handling.
🛠️ How: Use test rigs, measure flow, record readings.
🧰 Equipment: Venturimeter, Pelton wheel, pump test rig, manometers.
🛡️ Safety: Spill control, electrical safety near water, wear non‑slip shoes.
💼 Career: Fluid systems engineer, CFD analyst.

🔬 Curies Metallurgy Lab

Material Science & Testing
📌 What? Microstructure, hardness, tensile testing, heat treatment.
⚡ Need: Select materials, failure analysis, quality control.
🧩 Applications: Aerospace, automotive, manufacturing.
✅ Advantages: Understand material behaviour. ⚠️ Disadvantages: sample preparation.
🛠️ How: Polish specimen, etch, observe under microscope, perform tests.
🧰 Equipment: UTM, Rockwell hardness, muffle furnace, metallurgical microscope.
🛡️ Safety: Hot surfaces, chemical etchants, wear gloves & goggles.
💼 Career: Metallurgist, quality engineer, failure analyst.

⚡ Newton's Machine Lab

Theory of Machines & Mechanisms
📌 What? Kinematics, dynamics, vibrations, gyroscope, balancing.
⚡ Need: Design of linkages, cams, vibration control.
🧩 Applications: Engine mechanisms, robotics, suspension.
✅ Advantages: Tangible understanding of motion. ⚠️ Disadvantages: setup complexity.
🛠️ How: Assemble mechanisms, measure displacement, velocity.
🧰 Equipment: Four‑bar linkage, cam follower, gyroscope, vibration shaker.
🛡️ Safety: Rotating parts, secure guards, avoid loose clothing.
💼 Career: Design engineer, robotics, mechanical analyst.

🔥 Maxwell Lab – Thermal Lab 2

Heat Transfer & Refrigeration
📌 What? Conduction, convection, radiation, heat exchangers, refrigeration.
⚡ Need: Design of cooling systems, HVAC, power plants.
🧩 Applications: Radiators, chillers, thermal management.
✅ Advantages: Real thermal data. ⚠️ Disadvantages: high temperatures.
🛠️ How: Set up experiment, record temperatures, calculate coefficients.
🧰 Equipment: Shell & tube HX, Stefan‑Boltzmann app, refrigeration trainer.
🛡️ Safety: Hot surfaces, refrigerant handling, use gloves.
💼 Career: Thermal engineer, HVAC designer.

🛡️ Safety: Ventilation, hearing protection, no open flames.

🏛️ About Mechanical Engineering · Sanjivani

The Department of Mechanical Engineering at Sanjivani College of Engineering, Kopargaon, established in 1983, offers a comprehensive curriculum with 11 specialised laboratories. Our labs are equipped with modern instruments and machines to provide hands‑on experience in design, thermal, fluid, manufacturing, and automation. We emphasise practical learning, industry collaboration, and innovation. Every lab is designed to nurture problem‑solving skills and prepare students for careers in engineering, research, and entrepreneurship.
